A new agent specialized in manuscript fact-checking — cross-referencing the prose against the story bible, character profiles, and timeline to find continuity errors. Character eye color changes between chapters. The drive from City A to City B takes 3 hours in Chapter 4 but 6 hours in Chapter 14. A minor character is introduced as left-handed in Chapter 2 and plays guitar right-handed in Chapter 19. Lumen doesn't catch these — Lumen reads structure, not canon. Sable doesn't either — Sable reads mechanics, not continuity.

The deliverable is a continuity report (`source/continuity-report.md`) that lists potential inconsistencies with chapter references. The author reviews each and decides: genuine error, or intentional? The agent never makes that call. It only surfaces discrepancies. Fits naturally between Second Assessment and Copy Edit as an optional phase, or as an on-demand tool accessible via Hot Take-style button.

This is especially valuable for series authors — Book 2 needs a continuity check against Book 1's established facts, which is exactly the kind of cross-volume problem the series bible can't fully solve on its own. For standalone novels, any book with a complex cast, world-building, or non-linear time will benefit. Call it "Lore" or "Archivist." It reads everything and writes one report.

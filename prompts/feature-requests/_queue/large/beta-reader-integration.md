Almost every serious author uses beta readers — people who read the manuscript and give feedback before professional editing. Right now that feedback is completely outside the Novel Engine pipeline. You get a Word doc full of comments back from your beta reader and have to manually translate it into something Forge can work with. This feature closes that loop.

Two parts: export and import. Export: a "Beta Reader Package" from Build view — a clean formatted PDF or DOCX with chapter numbers and page breaks, plus an optional structured feedback template (character reactions, pacing notes, confusion points, favorites). Import: paste or upload the feedback text, and Forge runs in a new "Beta Feedback Mode" that synthesizes multiple reader responses into a revision plan, identifying patterns across readers and producing project-tasks.md and revision-prompts.md just like Direct Feedback Mode.

This doesn't require magical AI accuracy — Forge just needs the raw feedback text and can do what it already does with author notes. The export side is mostly already possible via the Pandoc build. The import side is a new Forge mode. Should work entirely offline. Beta reader feedback is one of the most important inputs in the revision process and it currently has no home in the pipeline at all.

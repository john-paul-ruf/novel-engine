A per-chapter focused analysis mode: select any chapter and get a surgical craft assessment of that single scene. Not Lumen's full-manuscript structural sweep — just this chapter. Is the opening line doing its job? Where does tension spike and go flat? Is the scene opening and closing on different emotional territory (does something change)? How much of the scene is action vs. interiority vs. dialogue, and is the balance right for what the scene needs to do?

This would be Lumen operating in a scoped mode — load the target chapter, load its notes.md if present, load the scene outline entry for that chapter, and deliver a focused craft analysis without reading the whole manuscript first. Faster than a full assessment, more actionable than generic feedback. Useful during drafting (is this chapter working before I move on?) and during revision (is the fix actually better than what was there before?).

A "Deep Dive" button in the FilesView chapter toolbar, or a Quick Action on Lumen's chat. Output stays in chat — no files written. No pipeline phase gating. This should be available at any point during the project: during first draft, during revision, after copy edit if a chapter still feels off. The full Lumen assessment is a project milestone; the chapter deep dive is a tool for any day you're uncertain about a scene.

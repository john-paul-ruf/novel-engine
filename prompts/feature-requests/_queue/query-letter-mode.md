Quill is excellent at Amazon KDP packaging — book descriptions, keywords, BISAC codes. But a large portion of authors (especially those writing literary fiction, upmarket commercial, or anything that isn't pure genre) are querying literary agents, not self-publishing. The traditional publishing path requires a completely different package: a one-page query letter, a one-page synopsis, and sometimes a longer synopsis. These formats have their own conventions and their own failure modes.

A Quill sub-mode (or a new step at the end of the pipeline) would produce traditional publishing documents. The pitch card from Spark, the story bible, the voice profile, and the author profile already contain everything Quill needs. The query letter is ~250 words: hook, premise, stakes, brief bio, comp titles. The synopsis is a full plot summary with no withholding — agents want to know how it ends. Both are genres that most authors struggle with enormously and usually pay someone to help them with.

The simplest implementation: a "Publishing Route" toggle in the Quill session — "Self-Publishing" (current behavior) or "Traditional Publishing." The traditional route produces query-letter.md and synopsis.md in addition to metadata.md. No pipeline changes needed. Quill already has everything; it just needs different output instructions.
